FAQs

  1. What is the best time of year to visit Islamabad?
    The best time to visit Islamabad is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

  2. What are some must-visit attractions in Islamabad?
    Islamabad offers a range of attractions, including Faisal Mosque, Pakistan Monument, Daman-e-Koh viewpoint, Lok Virsa Museum, and Rawal Lake, among others.

  3. Is Islamabad a safe destination for tourists?
    Islamabad is generally considered a safe city for tourists. However, like any other destination, it's essential to exercise common-sense safety measures and stay informed about any travel advisories.

  4. Is Islamabad a safe destination for tourists?

  5. What is the local currency in Islamabad, and where can I exchange money?
    The local currency in Pakistan is the Pakistani Rupee (PKR). You can exchange money at banks, currency exchange offices, and even some hotels in Islamabad.

  6. What are some local dishes or cuisines to try in Islamabad?
    Islamabad offers a wide range of Pakistani dishes, such as biryani, nihari, and kebabs. Be sure to try local street food and explore the vibrant food scene.

  7. What language is commonly spoken in Islamabad, and is English widely understood?
    Urdu is the official language, but English is also widely understood, especially in urban areas. You can easily communicate in English with many locals.

  8. How can I get around Islamabad and explore the city?
    Islamabad has a reliable public transportation system, including buses and taxis. Ride-sharing apps are also available. Renting a car is an option, but driving conditions may be challenging for newcomers.
